Fund 'Em When They're Young?: Does Better Funding in Elementary and Secondary School Improve High School Graduation Rates?

By Lux Hogan-Murphy and Clifford Stowe

Louisiana

Iowa Missouri
This infographic shows a correlation between increased spending per student and graduation rate. The lowest graduation rate corresponds with the lowest spending, and vice versa (although there are two districts with different spendings and the same graduation rate). Compared to the infographic of the entire nation, California has an upward trend in the numerical value of graduation rates relative to spending per student that is not visible in the national infographic. This would suggest that this trend is isolated and is not representative of the nation if states are compared. when examining data for the country.
We compared school funding data from 2012 to graduation rate data from 2019 to see if better funding in elementary school contributed to higher graduation rates for those children, and found that there was too much variety from state to state to draw any national conclusion, although there are noticeable patterns within states, and the different patterns we believe could be a result of each state's policies regarding assigning funding.
